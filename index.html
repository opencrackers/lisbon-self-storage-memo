Investment Memo · Greater Lisbon · May 2026

Self-storage works here — but only if you buy the box cheap.

For a 2,000 sqm+ first site around Cascais/Sintra or Almada/Caparica, the edge is not land or a new build. It is an underpriced warehouse conversion with clean use permissions, road visibility, and enough catchment to reach the low-80s occupancy without heavy staffing.

Base verdict: pursue only if all-in cost lands below roughly €1,500 / gross sqm (≈ €2,300 / net rentable sqm at 65% efficiency, or about €1,150 / net rentable of equity at 50% debt). The harder test added below: your yield-on-cost must clear the exit cap rate, or the deal cash-flows but destroys value on a trading basis. At today's costs that gate is tighter than it looks.

01 — Critical Thesis

Where the opportunity is, and where it isn't

Where I see opportunity

  • Cascais fringe / Abóboda / Alcabideche / São Domingos de Rana: strong household wealth, expat/renovation/seasonal demand, and no dense storage coverage in Cascais town itself.
  • Sintra line near IC19/A16: cheaper industrial stock, dense population, small-business demand — but weaker premium pricing than Cascais, and an incumbent already in Massamá (see correction in Competitors).
  • Caparica / Monte de Caparica: possible gap for residents, seasonal operators, surf/tourism, students, trades. Needs a careful competition check because Almada/Cacilhas is already served.

Where I would be careful

  • Buying land to build new: too slow and uncertain for a first site. Planning and construction can erase the operating-leverage benefit.
  • Prime industrial warehouses: if it's good enough for logistics users, you compete with logistics rents and yields. Storage only wins if retail-storage rent beats industrial rent after conversion cost.
  • Central-Lisbon logic in the suburbs: headline online prices look high, but large units and discounting pull the blended rate down.

Go condition

All-in < €1,500/gross sqm, zoning/use already storage-compatible, fire-safety route clear, ≥1,300 sqm net rentable from 2,000 sqm gross — and yield-on-cost above the stabilized exit cap.

No-go condition

Acquisition above €1,600–1,800/gross sqm before fit-out, weak road visibility, no 24/7 access, or any speculative use-class fight with the município.

Best first move

Lease- or purchase-option a neglected warehouse for 6–12 months while you pre-sell units, validate search demand, and complete licensing/fire diligence.

02 — Market Logic

Demand is real; the gap is convenient suburban supply

Demand drivers

  • Expensive housing and small flats create persistent overflow-storage demand.
  • Renovations, moving, separations, expat relocation, and seasonal property use drive short-term consumer demand.
  • E-commerce sellers, trades, restaurants, events, and tourism operators drive small-business demand.
  • Suburban car access matters more than central foot traffic for large units.

Supply signal — and the missing number

Coverage is uneven: Cascais town looks thin, Box2Box says residents there use Oeiras/Algés/Lisbon. But the memo never sizes demand. The core self-storage feasibility metric is rentable sqm of supply per capita in a 3–5 km catchment. Europe averages a fraction of US penetration and Portugal sits near the bottom — which supports the thesis. Quantify one catchment before any offer.

Interpretation: the gap is not "Lisbon has no storage." It is convenient suburban storage with clean access and credible pricing in a specific, measured micro-catchment.

04 — International Operating Models

What "good" looks like at scale

US REIT model

Public Storage / Extra Space scale through ownership, revenue management, and national marketing. Corrected PSA ran ~92% occupancy and a ~78% same-store NOI margin in 2025 — not 75%.

Mature benchmark: high, and not realistic for a first Portugal site.

European operator model

Shurgard, Bluespace and peers chase dense urban/suburban catchments, premium security, and web-led acquisition. FEDESSA/CBRE 2025: European rates ≈ €312/sqm/yr (~€26/mo), occupancy ~81%. Europe is under-penetrated vs the US.

Target margin for a small Portuguese site: 55–65% NOI stabilized.

Low-staff automation model

Online booking, remote KYC, smart locks, CCTV, digital contracts, card billing, call-centre support. Best fit for a simple first site where customers self-access.

Operationally lean — but front-loaded capex and tech discipline matter.

08 — Portugal-Specific Risk

What the model leaves out — and what to verify

Licensing & fire safety

Conversion can trigger municipal use-class, building-works, accessibility, and SCIE/fire requirements. First diligence gate — get a fire engineer's memo per candidate.

VAT & acquisition tax

23% VAT on storage as a service (recoverable on capex, charged on rent); IMT ~6.5% + 0.8% stamp on the buy. Folded into the underwriter's transaction-cost line — confirm classification per site.

Ramp-up risk

The site can look bad for 12–24 months. Debt service starts at close; occupancy doesn't. The lease-up reserve above is the equity that covers this — size leverage for a slow fill.

Exit / value creation

If yield-on-cost doesn't beat the stabilized exit cap, the deal cash-flows but loses value on a trade. The base case shows how tight this is at today's costs.

Marketing & CAC

Fill depends on website, search, reviews, dynamic pricing, conversion. A hidden site with weak SEO won't fill just because demand exists.

Customer mix

Consumers pay higher €/sqm but churn; business takes larger space at lower €/sqm but stabilises occupancy. Target ~55–65% consumer, 35–45% business.

09 — What Would Be Needed

Validation before any offer

Deal-screening checklist

  • 2,000–4,000 sqm gross or expandable industrial site.
  • ≥60–70% conversion to net rentable.
  • Direct van access, loading, parking, 24/7 gate.
  • Dry, secure, no major roof/flood/humidity issue.
  • Existing warehouse/industrial use — not residential grey zone.
  • All-in below €1,500/gross sqm after works & transaction costs.

Validation before offer

  • Call 8–12 competitors as a customer for real quotes, discounts, waiting lists.
  • Run Google Ads test landing pages for Cascais/Sintra/Almada/Caparica to measure lead cost.
  • Get a fast SCIE/use-class memo per candidate site.
  • Get bank term sheets at 50%, 55%, 60% LTC.
  • Pre-sell business units to trades/e-commerce/tourism before closing.
  • Size the catchment: sqm/capita vs existing supply within 3–5 km.

Final recommendation

Don't start by buying raw land. Hunt a mispriced warehouse/factory in Sintra/Cacém/Rio de Mouro or the Cascais fringe — ideally with an option structure — and validate demand before full capex. Caparica is interesting only if the site is cheap and you can prove a local underserved base; central Almada is already too contested unless the building is exceptional.

The opportunity is real, but it's a real-estate acquisition game first and an operating business second. The winner is whoever buys the right box at the right basis — and clears the exit cap.
